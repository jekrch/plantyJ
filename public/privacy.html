Privacy Policy

PlantyJ · Last updated 22 July 2026

PlantyJ stores your garden in your own Google Drive. It keeps no database of your plants, photos, or notes on its own servers. The few narrow exceptions below exist only for features you choose to turn on.

Who we are

PlantyJ (“the app,” “we”) is a personal gardening app available at plantyj.com. This policy explains what data the app accesses through your Google Account, where it goes, and what, if anything, is retained. Questions: jekrch@gmail.com.

Google Account data we access

When you sign in with Google, the app requests these scopes:

  • openid, email, profile: your name, email address, profile picture, and Google account identifier, used to show who is signed in and to identify your garden.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.file, a per-file Drive scope that lets the app create and manage only the files it creates (your PlantyJ garden folder). It cannot see, read, or touch any other file in your Drive.

Your Google sign-in details and access token are held in your browser for the duration of your session and are not transmitted to or stored on any PlantyJ server.

Where your garden lives

Everything you create in PlantyJ (plants, photos, notes, zones, your display name and avatar) is written to a folder in your own Google Drive. PlantyJ does not run a database, does not keep a server-side copy of your garden, and cannot read your garden except through your browser using the access you granted.

Optional features that involve our servers

The base app is entirely between your browser and your Drive. Two opt-in sharing features route data through a lightweight proxy (a Cloudflare Worker). Neither is used unless you choose it.

1. Sharing a garden publicly

If you use “Share publicly,” you grant anyone-with-the-link read access to that garden’s Drive files, and our proxy fetches those files and briefly caches them at Cloudflare’s edge so they load quickly for visitors. Only files belonging to a garden you deliberately publish are ever fetched this way. Cloudflare may log request metadata (such as IP address and timestamp) as part of operating the service. Choosing “Stop sharing” revokes the public access immediately; cached copies expire shortly after.

2. Custom share links

If you claim a custom link (for example plantyj.com/?u=your-name), we store a small mapping so the link can resolve. This is the only personal data PlantyJ retains on its own servers, and only for users who claim a link. It consists of:

  • the username you chose;
  • the Drive file ID of your published garden’s manifest;
  • your Google account identifier (the opaque sub), used only to prove you own the link so no one else can take or change it.

No email, name, photo, or garden content is stored in this mapping. To verify ownership when you claim a link, your access token is checked once against Google’s userinfo endpoint; the token itself is not stored.

Third-party botanical lookups

The optional “Enrich my garden” feature looks up plant species names against public botanical databases: GBIF, Wikipedia, and Kew’s Plants of the World Online. Only the species name is sent; no personal information or account data is included in these requests.

AI-assisted drafting features generate text prompts for you to copy and use in a model of your choice. The app does not automatically transmit your garden data to any AI provider.

What we do not do

  • We do not sell or rent your data.
  • We do not use it for advertising or profiling.
  • We do not use Google user data to develop, improve, or train generalized AI/ML models.
  • We do not share Google user data with third parties except as needed to run a feature you enabled (as described above).

PlantyJ’s use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Retention and deletion

Because your garden lives in your Drive, you control it directly. In the app, “Delete my garden” permanently removes the PlantyJ folder from your Drive and revokes the app’s access. You can also revoke access anytime at myaccount.google.com/permissions.

A custom-link mapping is released when you change or delete it, or on request. To have any retained mapping removed, email jekrch@gmail.com.

Children

PlantyJ is not directed to children under 13.
